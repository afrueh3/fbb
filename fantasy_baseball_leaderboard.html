How the combined stats are calculated
  • Counting stats (GP, R, HR, RBI, SB, QS, SV+H) are summed directly across seasons.
  • H/AB are extracted from the per-season "H/AB" cell and summed.
  • SLG: per-season total bases = SLG × AB. Combined SLG = ΣTB / ΣAB.
  • K: per-season strikeouts = (K/9) × IP / 9. Combined K is the rounded sum.
  • ERA: per-season earned runs = ERA × IP / 9. Combined ERA = (ΣER × 9) / ΣIP.
  • WHIP: per-season walks+hits = WHIP × IP. Combined WHIP = Σ(W+H) / ΣIP.
  • IP is parsed in baseball notation: .1 = 1/3 inning, .2 = 2/3 inning. Sums are formatted back the same way.
  • OBP is omitted because the source page doesn't expose plate appearances or walks.
  • Position info is sourced from Yahoo's 2026 team log for currently-rostered players, supplemented with manually-provided historical eligibility for older-roster players. Each player's positions are the union across any season they appeared (e.g., Ceddanne Rafaela shows 2B, OF, SS, so any of the three position filters includes him).
  • Bold names are players currently on the active 2026 roster (refreshed daily by the scheduled task), matching the convention used by Baseball Reference for active players.
  • Per-season values are rounded by Yahoo before display, so combined ER/WH/K can differ by a small fraction from a perfect calculation done on raw counts. For a player who played a meaningful number of innings, the deviation is well under 1%.
